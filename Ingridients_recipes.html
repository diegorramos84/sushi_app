Types of Sushi

Sashimi

Simply put, sashimi is raw fish (occasionally cooked) sliced thin and popularly served without accompanying dishes but normally with dipping sauces based on - for instance - soy, wasabi and ginger.

Maki

Maki or Makizushi, refers to “rolled” sushi rice. The rice is rolled in a sheet of dry seaweed, called “nori.”

Nigiri

Nigiri is a type of sushi that combines a pillow of vinegared rice with a topping of raw or cooked seafood. These two ingredients are all that's needed to create a perfect match of flavors and textures.

Uramaki

Uramaki in Japanese means inside out, meaning the seaweed is inside the roll.
